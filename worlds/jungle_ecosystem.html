Section 06
Sub-Bass Anatomy
Below 80Hz lies the physical territory of jungle — frequencies felt in the chest and stomach before they register as pitch. Explore the full spectrum and sub-bass cross-section of a simulated jungle mix.
0 dB
30 Hz
Sub-bass below 80Hz is felt more than heard at high SPL. At club volume, these frequencies cause physical resonance in the chest cavity — jungle producers exploited this as an emotional and bodily tool.
Jungle mixes sub-bass in mono for maximum speaker compatibility. Stereo bass below 100Hz causes phase cancellation on mono systems, losing the entire low-end — pirate radio demanded mono-safe mixes.
The Reese bass lives at 60–120Hz — the chest-pressure zone. A detuned oscillator pair creates a slow beating frequency that modulates at 1–4Hz, producing the distinctive "breathing" throb of jungle and early drum & bass.
